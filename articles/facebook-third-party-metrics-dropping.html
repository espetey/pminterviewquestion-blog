You are the PM of Facebook 3rd Party Login, and you see your numbers are declining 2% each week. What do you do?

Friday, July 10, 2020

I'm following the trend of doing a question that comes off as company-specific at first but should be a little more generalizable after a little brainstorming. In this case, while it would help to know the ins and outs of Facebook's specific product, I would start from first principles around software development: new code, bugs, and network performance. Knowing how to begin tracking those issues down doesn't require a lot of Facebook-specific knowledge and can help to check off a lot of boxes. The scenario that a new competitor has begun stealing our customers is always a possibility but will take a little more external research, while code/bugs/network should be discoverable with a few calls to the devops, engineering, and QA teams.

If usage numbers were declining 2% each week, I would first consider what the cause is. I would start with three possibilities:

  1. Our service level has degraded to the point where users are dropping off
  2. Our product has a bug in the workflow that is preventing successful logins
  3. A competitor has entered the space

I would assess whether there is a service or network issue first, as this would generally be a data gathering mission and would be a quick way to rule out anything that could potentially be fixed with a call to the devops team. To do so, I would gather recent network performance metrics, such as average network response times and failed requests, and compare them with the same metrics from previous weeks and months. I would be looking for any trends to suggest that performance had been decreasing leading up to this point. I would also look for anomalies in the performance that might suggest performance issues that may have been rising and falling prior to our discovery of a 2% decline in usage.

My second question would be whether there is a bug in the workflow that is preventing successful logins. Investigating this would involve a few things. First I would look into the specific traffic that has decreased: Have we lost only iOS users? Or only users in one region? This could help isolate whether a bug was introduced into a specific code stream, such as a web- or mobile-focused API. It could also help identify if the decrease was related to an A/B test that had been recently performed. I would also work with an engineer to look at recent code commits to learn if anything was pushed in a similar timeframe as the decline's beginning. Second, I would talk to the QA team to learn if they've noticed anything that has changed with quality of the service.

After feeling relatively certain it is not related to any internal issues, I would begin to do some market research to learn if there have been any new entrants into the 3rd-party login space or if a known competitor has recently released a significant upgrade or feature set. I would also take to social media and programming channels like Stack Overflow to learn if developers are discussing problems with Facebook's offering or if a competitor is being discussed more than they had in the past.
