How do you make the Twitter user experience feel more secure after last week's hack?

Monday, July 20, 2020

I just want to preface this answer with the statement that I believe Twitter handled the hacking situation last week very well, at least from my non-blue-badge perspective. All the official Twitter accounts lit up with notifications and post-mortem-style tweet threads. Jack was Tweeting as well and even had a Tweet of solidarity, "Tough day for us at Twitter. We all feel terrible this happened." At any rate, security is hard and at scale I don't know if any digital platform is invulnerable. The best thing to do, in my opinion, is over communicate and show your users you're doing something about it.
